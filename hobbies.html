RUNNING

Running is the sport I have been doing since I was 20 years old. Being in a very sporty engineering school I wanted to try a sport that is easy to do. During my first race, after one kilometre, I was out of breath. At the age of 20, this was a turning point for me. How can I be young and unable to run? Since that day I have not stopped except for injuries. I don't do it for competitive purposes. It simply allows me to breathe after a busy day and to surpass myself day after day, overcoming barriers. It also makes me feel more revitalised and productive during the day.

HULAHOOP

After injuring my knees while running, I saw the hulahoop challenge on social media. The goal was simply to practice hulahooping for 30 minutes every day. It seemed like a good cardio alternative to running. I was not wrong! My abdominal, leg and arm muscles became stronger. My body became toned very quickly.
